Group Project for OutFront Minnesota: Web Navigation and Accessibility Update

Project Overview


Problem:

Many young adults in Minnesota feel isolated from their local LGBTQ+ community because they don't have the resources to connect, learn about and rally around the important issues regarding LGBTQ+ people's rights and safety.

The Solution:

We believe that by updating certain aspects of the OutFront MN website we will be able to encourage community members to check and use the website more often so that they can learn and connect more with the LGBTQ+ community and their counterparts.

Created by:

Malinda Carisch, Sara Hudis, Elise Mary Lanigan

Tools:

Figma, Miro, Invision

  • Our goals for the site based off of our Heuristic Evaluation
  • Changing the layout to make content easier to look at and navigate
  • Improving text accessibility and consistency especially on the website’s buttons
  • Maintaining visual hierarchy/ better clarity on headings and subheadings. Draw the viewer’s eye to the most important page elements
  • Simplifying menu options. Condense top menu from two bars to one.

Interview and Survey Key Takeaways

  • Over half of our respondents felt like they were not at all involved with their community.
  • Most participants had interacted with nonprofits in the past through donating or visiting organization websites
  • Most participants learned about the nonprofits they support through people they know.
  • Many of our interviews and survey participants felt like they were not as involved with the LGBTQ+ community as they would like to be.

User Persona


Definition & Synthesis

OutFront MN offers its audience access to information and resources that are available on a local level making their content more relevant and easier to implement

What sets OutFront apart is the local community involvement and focusing on the most prevalent issues facing the community here in Minnesota.


Ideation

Laura is a Minnesota educator who wants to be more involved with and learn more about the LGBTQ+ community so that she can be a better resource for her students and community.

After learning about OutFront MN and going on their website, Laura is able to find both resources for her low-income students and educational resources on the LGBTQ+ community while also putting together a lesson plan for her students.



Sketches

  • Combine education and training training
  • Change covid-19 resources to “resources”
